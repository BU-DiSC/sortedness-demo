Near-Sortedness in Indexes

Real-world data ingestion is rarely perfectly sorted — incoming records often arrive nearly sorted, with a small fraction entries displaced. What happens when such workloads are ingested into an index?
This demo lets you configure near-sorted data using the K-L-sortedness metric, visualize the data, and ingest such near-sorted streams into indexes that adapt to sortedness, as well as classical B+trees.
